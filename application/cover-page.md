## 250-Word Abstract

Every day, family and individual caregivers for veterans observe things that matter — a night without sleep, a mood shift, a missed appointment, a moment of crisis. Most of those observations are never captured. When a caregiver sits down with a VA provider or a doctor, they are asked to remember. What they cannot remember does not become part of the record. What is not in the record cannot inform care, support a claim, or protect the veteran.

CareLog is a browser-based AI tool that eliminates this documentation burden. The caregiver speaks or types naturally about their day — no forms, no clinical language, no structured input required. The AI reads the entry, extracts structured observations automatically, and builds a longitudinal record over time. When a concern repeats across multiple days, the tool flags it as a pattern. When urgent language appears — a mental health crisis, a physical emergency, or a safety threat — the appropriate resources appear immediately on screen, tailored to whether the patient is a veteran or civilian. At any point, the caregiver can generate a clean, clinician-ready summary to share with a provider, a family member, or a VA coordinator.

CareLog was designed by a retired U.S. Army Sergeant First Class who has navigated the military medical system from active duty through the VA — and who has seen firsthand what happens when observations are not captured at the point of care. The tool is currently at Technology Readiness Level 3: a working, demonstrable proof of concept, built and tested, ready for real-world validation.
